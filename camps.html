Basic Camp Details

Foundation camps for all NCC cadets — ATC & CATC

ATC – Annual Training Camp

Battalion Level | 12 Days

The Annual Training Camp is the most fundamental camp of NCC training. Conducted at Battalion level, it covers basic military training, drill, weapon handling, map reading, field craft, and physical fitness.

Training Syllabus:
  • Physical Training & Obstacle Course
  • Drill (Open/Close Order, Ceremonial)
  • Weapon Training (.22 Rifle Firing)
  • Map Reading & Field Signals
  • First Aid & Health & Hygiene
  • National Integration & Personality Development
Daily Routine:
0500 - Reveille | 0530 - PT | 0700 - Breakfast | 0800 - Drill | 1000 - Classes | 1300 - Lunch | 1400 - Practical | 1600 - Games | 1900 - Roll Call | 2100 - Lights Off
Eligibility: All cadets Mandatory for B/C Cert

CATC – Combined Annual Training Camp

Group/Directorate Level | 12 Days

CATC brings together cadets from multiple battalions for combined training. It provides exposure to inter-BN activities, competitions, and national integration through interactions with cadets from different states and backgrounds.

Key Activities:
  • Inter-BN Drill Competition
  • Combined Arms Demonstration
  • Cultural Programme & Group Dance
  • Best Cadet Competition
  • Firing Competition (Individual & Team)
  • National Integration Lectures
Competitions:
Best Cadet (SD/SW/JD/JW) | Best Drill Squad | Tent Pitching | Map Reading Race | Cross Country | Tug of War | Cultural Night Trophy
Eligibility: All cadets Selection for higher camps

Advance Camp Details

Advanced training for selected cadets — Leadership & Specialized Skills

Advanced Leadership Camp (ALC)

Directorate Level | 12 Days

ALC develops leadership qualities and advanced military skills in selected Senior Division/Wing cadets. Cadets learn to lead under pressure, make tactical decisions, and manage teams effectively.

Training Modules:
  • Leadership & Management Exercises
  • Tactical Operations & Strategy
  • Advanced Weapon Handling (7.62mm SLR)
  • Night Navigation & Patrolling
  • Communication & Radio Procedures
  • Group Planning Exercises (GPE)
Eligibility: 2nd/3rd Year SD/SW Selection: Based on CATC performance

Adventure Training Camp

Directorate/National Level | 7-10 Days

Designed to build courage, team spirit, and endurance. Cadets participate in challenging outdoor activities in difficult terrains. NER region camps are conducted in the Himalayan foothills and NE terrain.

Activities:
  • High Altitude Trekking (8000-12000 ft)
  • Rock Climbing & Rappelling
  • River Crossing & Rafting
  • Cycle Expedition
  • Parasailing & Para-jumping
  • Survival Techniques & Jungle Craft
Eligibility: B/C Cert holders Medical fitness mandatory

National Integration Camp (NIC)

National Level | 12 Days

NIC promotes national unity by bringing cadets from all 17 NCC Directorates across India together. Cadets experience different cultures, languages, and traditions while building lifelong bonds.

Key Features:
  • Inter-Directorate Cultural Exchange
  • State Presentations & Folk Dance
  • Heritage Visits & Excursions
  • Unity Relay & Group Activities
  • Panel Discussions on National Issues
  • Community Development Projects
Eligibility: Selected from CATC All India participation

Vayu Sainik Camp (VSC)

National Level | 10 Days

National level camp for Air Wing cadets. Features aeromodelling competitions, flying orientation, and air force familiarization. Top cadets get gliding experience at Air Force stations.

Activities:
  • Aeromodelling & Flight Simulation
  • Gliding & Microlite Flying
  • Air Force Station Visit
  • Aerospace Lectures
  • Inter-Directorate Competition
  • Best Air Cadet Selection
Eligibility: Air Wing cadets only Selection through Directorate

Special Entry Camps (RDC / TSC)

Prestigious national-level camps — Only the best cadets are selected

Republic Day Camp (RDC)

National Level | Delhi | Jan-Feb every year

The most prestigious camp of NCC. Selected cadets participate in the Republic Day Parade on Rajpath (Kartavya Path), New Delhi. Only 2-3 cadets from each Directorate are selected through a rigorous multi-stage selection process spanning 6+ months.

Selection Process:
  1. BN Level Selection (Inter-Company)
  2. Group HQ Level Camp
  3. Directorate Level (Pre-RDC Camp)
  4. Inter-Directorate Competition
  5. Final Selection at Delhi
Selection Criteria:
  • Drill perfection (40% weightage)
  • Physical fitness & turnout
  • GK & NCC knowledge
  • Personality & leadership
  • Minimum height: 5'4" (M) / 5'1" (F)
Benefits: PM Rally attendance, DG's Commendation Card, Direct entry for SSB interview, Certificate of Excellence, Life-changing experience

Thal Sainik Camp (TSC)

National Level | Delhi | Sep-Oct every year

TSC is the ultimate inter-directorate competition camp for Army Wing cadets. Directorates compete in various military-oriented competitions. NER has consistently performed well in obstacle courses and firing events.

Competitions:
  • Obstacle Course (Individual & Team)
  • Firing (.22 Rifle - Individual & Team)
  • Line Area Competition
  • Health & Hygiene
  • Map Reading Race
  • Best Cadet Competition
Selection Criteria:
  • Outstanding physical fitness
  • Firing expertise (20/20 grouping)
  • Obstacle course timing
  • Overall camp performance
  • Discipline & bearing
Benefits: DG's Commendation, National recognition, Certificate for C-Exam bonus marks, Direct SSB recommendation eligibility
Nau Sainik Camp (NSC)

National level camp for Naval Wing cadets. Boat pulling, seamanship & naval exercises.

All India Shooting Camp

National shooting competition. Top marksmen from each Directorate compete at national level.

Youth Exchange Programme (YEP)

International exposure — Selected cadets visit friendly nations (UK, Singapore, Russia, etc.)
